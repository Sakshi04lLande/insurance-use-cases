Credit Risk Assessment:
Applicants with credit scores below 600 are considered high credit risk.
Applicants with credit scores between 600 and 700 are considered moderate credit risk.
Applicants with credit scores above 700 are considered low credit risk.

Claims History Evaluation:
Applicants with more than two claims in the last three policy years should be carefully reviewed.
Frequent claims may indicate behavioral or environmental risk.
High claim severity increases underwriting risk regardless of claim frequency.

Occupation Risk Factors:
Occupations involving physical labor, hazardous environments, or driving duties carry higher underwriting risk.
Office-based and professional occupations generally carry lower underwriting risk.

Medical Risk Considerations:
Applicants with high medical risk indicators may require premium loading or coverage exclusions.
Low medical risk applicants are generally acceptable for standard underwriting terms.

Fraud & Compliance:
Any confirmed fraud flag should result in immediate rejection.
Applications with suspicious activity must undergo enhanced due diligence.

Final Decision Guidance:
Final underwriting decisions should consider a holistic view of applicant profile, claims behavior, and external risk indicators.
